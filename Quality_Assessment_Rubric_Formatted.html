Quality Assessment Rubric for Mixed-Methods Research

Comprehensive Evaluation Tool for Dissertation Proposals and Studies

HOW TO USE THIS RUBRIC

Purpose: Evaluate the quality and rigor of mixed-methods research designs and studies

Users:

  • Doctoral students (self-assessment)
  • Dissertation chairs (proposal evaluation)
  • Committee members (defense preparation)
  • Peer reviewers (manuscript review)

Scoring:

  • Rate each criterion on the scale provided
  • Calculate subscale and total scores
  • Use feedback sections for specific comments
  • Identify areas needing strengthening

SECTION 1: DESIGN QUALITY (30 points)

1.1 Design Appropriateness (10 points)

The mixed-methods design is clearly needed and justified

| Score | Criteria |

|-------|----------|

| 10 points | Exemplary: Compelling rationale for why mixed methods is essential; clear explanation of what each strand contributes that the other cannot; articulates specific added value of integration |

| 8-9 points | Strong: Good justification for mixed methods; explains purpose of each strand adequately; integration value mentioned |

| 6-7 points | Adequate: Basic justification present; some explanation of strand purposes; integration rationale unclear |

| 4-5 points | Weak: Minimal justification; mixed methods may be unnecessary; unclear why both needed |

| 0-3 points | Insufficient: No clear rationale; appears mixed methods used to "hedge bets"; single method would suffice |

| Score | Criteria |

| 4-5 points | Weak: Questionable design; sample size small; measure quality concerns; sampling issues; many unaddressed threats |

QUALITY CHECKLIST FOR COMMON ISSUES

Design Issues:

  • [ ] Mixed methods truly necessary or just convenient?
  • [ ] Design type appropriate for research questions?
  • [ ] Clear notation and design diagram provided?
  • [ ] Phases appropriately sequenced (if sequential)?

Quantitative Issues:

  • [ ] Sample size adequate and justified?
  • [ ] Measures valid and reliable?
  • [ ] Analysis matches research questions?
  • [ ] Assumptions will be checked?

Qualitative Issues:

  • [ ] Purposeful sampling appropriate and justified?
  • [ ] Data sources sufficient for trustworthy findings?
  • [ ] Systematic analysis process described?
  • [ ] Trustworthiness strategies comprehensive?

Integration Issues:

  • [ ] Clear integration strategy specified?
  • [ ] Joint displays planned?
  • [ ] Process for meta-inferences described?
  • [ ] Plan for handling divergent findings?

Practical Issues:

  • [ ] Timeline realistic?
  • [ ] Resources adequate?
  • [ ] Access to participants secured or likely?
  • [ ] IRB approval planned/obtained?
