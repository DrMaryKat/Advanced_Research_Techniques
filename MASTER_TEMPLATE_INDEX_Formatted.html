üí° PRO TIPS FOR USING TEMPLATES

Do's ‚úÖ

DO customize templates for your context

  • Replace all bracketed text
  • Delete instructional notes
  • Add sections if needed
  • Use discipline-specific language

DO share with your chair early

  • Get feedback on structure
  • Confirm design choices
  • Align on expectations
  • Iterate based on input

DO use checklists religiously

  • Check off items as completed
  • Return to missed items
  • Use for progress tracking
  • Share with chair at meetings

DO combine templates strategically

  • Joint displays in Chapter 4
  • Methodology template in Chapter 3
  • Proposal becomes Chapters 1-3
  • Rubric for self-assessment throughout

Don'ts ‚ùå

DON'T submit templates with instructions still in them

  • Remove all italicized instructions
  • Delete placeholder text
  • Replace brackets with your content
  • Make it look professional

DON'T follow templates blindly

  • Adapt to your research
  • Check with committee requirements
  • Add what's missing for your field
  • Remove what doesn't apply

DON'T skip the planning tools

  • Decision tree and flowchart save time
  • Checklists prevent mistakes
  • Planning prevents problems
  • Shortcuts create setbacks

DON'T work in isolation

  • Use templates WITH your chair
  • Get peer feedback
  • Attend dissertation groups
  • Consult methodology experts

ü§ù GETTING HELP

When to Consult Your Chair

  • Before finalizing design (use flowchart/tree first, then discuss)
  • After completing proposal template (before investing heavily)
  • When stuck (any point where you're uncertain)
  • For major revisions (based on rubric feedback)

When to Consult a Statistician

  • Power analysis (sample size determination)
  • Complex MLM models (3+ levels, complex random effects)
  • Assumption violations (when robustness concerns arise)
  • Software questions (syntax, output interpretation)

When to Use Peer Support

  • Template feedback (have peer review using rubric)
  • Writing groups (accountability and feedback)
  • Practice presentations (proposal and defense prep)
  • Moral support (dissertation is marathon not sprint)
