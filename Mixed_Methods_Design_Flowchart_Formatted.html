DETAILED DECISION GUIDE

üîÑ Choose EXPLORATORY SEQUENTIAL (QUAL ‚Üí quan) if:

You need to:

  • ‚úì Develop an instrument because none exists for your context
  • ‚úì Identify variables or constructs that haven't been measured
  • ‚úì Generate a model or theory to test
  • ‚úì Understand a phenomenon before measuring it broadly

Your situation:

  • Little existing research in this area
  • Existing measures don't fit your population/context
  • Need to hear from stakeholders before developing survey
  • Want to ground quantitative work in lived experiences

Example Research Questions:

  • "What are the dimensions of culturally responsive leadership?" (Explore ‚Üí Measure)
  • "How do teachers experience principal feedback?" (Understand ‚Üí Quantify)

Timeline: Sequential - Qualitative phase MUST complete before quantitative begins

Typical Duration: 18-24 months for dissertation

Integration Point: Building phase - qual themes become quan measures

Strengths:

  • Ensures your instrument is contextually relevant
  • Gives voice to participants in measure development
  • Builds theory from ground up

Challenges:

  • Time intensive (two complete studies)
  • Need strong qualitative skills first
  • Quantitative phase depends on qualitative findings

üìä Choose EXPLANATORY SEQUENTIAL (QUAN ‚Üí qual) if:

You need to:

  • ‚úì Explain unexpected quantitative results
  • ‚úì Understand mechanisms behind statistical patterns
  • ‚úì Explore outlier cases in depth
  • ‚úì Add context and meaning to numbers

Your situation:

  • Starting with existing data or surveys
  • Have specific hypotheses to test first
  • Want to understand "how" and "why" after seeing "what"
  • Can identify interesting cases after quantitative analysis

Example Research Questions:

  • "Does mentoring improve retention, and how does it work?" (Measure ‚Üí Explain)
  • "Why did the intervention succeed in some schools but not others?" (Pattern ‚Üí Mechanism)

Timeline: Sequential - Quantitative phase guides qualitative phase

Typical Duration: 18-24 months for dissertation

Integration Point: Connecting phase - quan results inform qual sampling/questions

Strengths:

  • Quantitative provides direction for qualitative inquiry
  • Efficient use of qualitative resources (targeted sampling)
  • Strong explanation of statistical findings

Challenges:

  • Time intensive (two complete studies)
  • Can't start qualitative until quantitative is analyzed
  • May find nothing surprising to explain quantitatively

‚öñÔ∏è Choose CONVERGENT (QUAN + qual) if:

You need to:

  • ‚úì Triangulate findings from different sources
  • ‚úì Compare survey responses with lived experiences
  • ‚úì Validate one data source with another
  • ‚úì Develop comprehensive understanding from multiple angles

Your situation:

  • Resources to collect both types of data simultaneously
  • Want to see if findings converge, diverge, or expand
  • Need both breadth and depth at same time
  • Have questions best answered by both approaches equally

Example Research Questions:

  • "What is teacher morale and what factors influence it?" (Survey + Interviews together)
  • "How do students experience inclusive practices?" (Assessments + Focus groups)

Timeline: Concurrent - Both phases happen at same time

Typical Duration: 12-18 months for dissertation (more efficient than sequential)

Integration Point: Analysis/interpretation - Merge findings in joint displays

Strengths:

  • More efficient than sequential designs
  • Direct comparison of findings
  • Handles divergent findings through integration

Challenges:

  • Resource intensive (collecting both simultaneously)
  • Need team or strong skills in both methods
  • Integration can be complex
  • Must plan both strands upfront

üîó Choose EMBEDDED (QUAN(qual) or QUAL(quan)) if:

You need to:

  • ‚úì Support a primary design with supplemental data
  • ‚úì Address different research questions at different levels
  • ‚úì Enhance understanding without full second study
  • ‚úì Add context to primary approach

Your situation:

  • Clear primary method (quant OR qual)
  • Secondary method plays supporting role
  • Limited resources but want some mixed methods
  • Questions are hierarchical (main + subsidiary)

Example Research Questions:

  • Primary: "Does PD improve teaching practice?" Secondary: "How did teachers experience PD?" (RCT + embedded interviews)
  • Primary: "How do principals enact equity?" Secondary: "What is prevalence of observed practices?" (Ethnography + frequency counts)

Timeline: Concurrent or sequential - Depends on how embedded strand supports primary

Typical Duration: 12-18 months (less than full mixed methods)

Integration Point: Throughout - one strand supports the other continuously

Strengths:

  • More feasible than full mixed methods
  • Adds depth/breadth to primary design
  • Can be done by solo researcher

Challenges:

  • Risk of tokenism - embedded strand too small
  • Must clarify which method is primary
  • Limited integration compared to other designs

‚úä Choose TRANSFORMATIVE Design if:

Your research:

  • Centers social justice, equity, or empowerment
  • Involves marginalized or vulnerable communities
  • Aims to challenge power structures or advocate for change
  • Uses participatory or action research approaches

Any of the above designs can be transformative by:

  • Involving community members in all research phases
  • Centering marginalized voices and experiences
  • Using findings to advocate for systemic change
  • Conducting research WITH not ON participants
  • Examining power, privilege, and oppression explicitly

Example:

  • Convergent design exploring educational equity through student voices + achievement data
  • Explanatory sequential measuring opportunity gaps ‚Üí Understanding lived experiences of inequity

PRACTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

Time and Resources

| Design | Timeline | Resources Needed | Feasibility for Solo Researcher |

|--------|----------|-----------------|-------------------------------|

| Exploratory Sequential | 18-24 months | High (two complete studies) | Moderate (if skilled in both) |

| Explanatory Sequential | 18-24 months | High (two complete studies) | Moderate (if skilled in both) |

| Convergent | 12-18 months | Very High (simultaneous) | Challenging (may need team) |

| Embedded | 12-18 months | Moderate (primary + support) | Good (manageable scope) |

Skill Requirements

You need strong skills in:

  • Sequential designs: Your FIRST method (must complete before second)
  • Convergent: BOTH methods simultaneously
  • Embedded: Your PRIMARY method primarily

Consider:

  • Your methodological training and comfort
  • Available mentorship and consultation
  • Learning curve for unfamiliar methods
  • Committee expertise and expectations

DESIGN NOTATION GUIDE

Use standard notation in your proposal/dissertation:

Notation System:

  • UPPERCASE = Priority or emphasis
  • lowercase = Less emphasis or supplementary
  • + = Concurrent (at same time)
  • ‚Üí = Sequential (one after the other)
  • ( ) = Embedded within

Examples:

  • QUAL ‚Üí quan = Exploratory Sequential (qual drives quan)
  • QUAN ‚Üí qual = Explanatory Sequential (quan drives qual)
  • QUAN + qual = Convergent (equal emphasis)
  • QUAN(qual) = Embedded (qual within quant)

COMMON MISTAKES IN DESIGN SELECTION

‚ùå Mistake 1: Choosing Mixed Methods to "Hedge Your Bets"

  • Wrong reason: "If one method doesn't work, I'll have the other"
  • Right reason: "I need both to fully answer my questions"

‚ùå Mistake 2: Adding Token Qualitative or Quantitative Component

  • Inadequate: 3 interviews added to large survey study (tokenism)
  • Adequate: 30 interviews providing genuine insight to explain survey patterns

‚ùå Mistake 3: No Real Integration

  • Not mixed methods: Running parallel studies with no connection
  • True mixed methods: Connecting, merging, or embedding with clear integration strategy

‚ùå Mistake 4: Wrong Sequential Order

  • Don't: Start with quantitative then use interviews to develop questions (backwards!)
  • Do: Match sequence to purpose (explore first if developing measures)

‚ùå Mistake 5: Ignoring Pragmatics

  • Unrealistic: Solo doctoral student planning convergent design with 500 surveys + 100 interviews
  • Realistic: Solo student doing explanatory sequential with 200 surveys ‚Üí 20 targeted interviews
