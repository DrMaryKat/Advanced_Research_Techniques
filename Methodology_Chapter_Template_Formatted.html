Model Building Sequence:

Describe the sequence of models you'll estimate

Step 1: Null Model (Unconditional Model)

The null model partitions variance in the outcome and calculates the intraclass correlation:

Level 1: Yij = Œ≤0j + rij
Level 2: Œ≤0j = Œ≥00 + u0j

Where:

  • Yij = outcome for individual i in group j
  • Œ≤0j = mean outcome for group j
  • Œ≥00 = grand mean across all groups
  • u0j = random effect for group j
  • rij = individual residual

Purpose: Establish need for multilevel modeling through ICC calculation. Determine variance at each level.

Step 2: Random Intercept Model with Level-1 Predictors

Level 1: Yij = Œ≤0j + Œ≤1j(X1ij) + Œ≤2j(X2ij) + rij
Level 2: Œ≤0j = Œ≥00 + u0j
         Œ≤1j = Œ≥10
         Œ≤2j = Œ≥20

Purpose: Examine Level-1 predictors while accounting for clustering. Fixed slopes assumed.

Step 3: Random Slopes Model

Level 1: Yij = Œ≤0j + Œ≤1j(X1ij) + Œ≤2j(X2ij) + rij
Level 2: Œ≤0j = Œ≥00 + u0j
         Œ≤1j = Œ≥10 + u1j
         Œ≤2j = Œ≥20 + u2j

Purpose: Test whether relationships vary across groups. Compare fit to random intercept model.

Step 4: Full Model with Level-2 Predictors and Cross-Level Interactions

Level 1: Yij = Œ≤0j + Œ≤1j(X1ij) + Œ≤2j(X2ij) + rij
Level 2: Œ≤0j = Œ≥00 + Œ≥01(W1j) + u0j
         Œ≤1j = Œ≥10 + Œ≥11(W1j) + u1j
         Œ≤2j = Œ≥20 + u2j

Purpose: Explain variance in slopes and intercepts using Level-2 predictors. Test cross-level interactions.

WRITING TIPS FOR CHAPTER 3

Voice and Tense

  • Use past tense for completed activities: "Data were collected..."
  • Use future tense if proposal stage: "Data will be collected..."
  • Use first person when appropriate: "I conducted interviews..."
  • Be precise and specific - avoid vagueness

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Insufficient detail (reader can't replicate your study)

‚ùå Mixing methodology with results

‚ùå Omitting justifications for decisions

‚ùå Vague language ("some participants," "data were analyzed")

‚ùå Missing citations for instruments/methods

‚ùå Inadequate discussion of validity/trustworthiness

‚ùå Failure to connect methods to research questions
